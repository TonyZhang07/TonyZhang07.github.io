Reflection

To the class in the future,

I was skeptical about this class at the beginning, especially the first post. It has brought me back horrible memories of a high-school teacher who refuses/hated to use technology in her class. That high-school teacher was also one of my English teachers as well, so you could see how that went. I was simply not excited to take this class, but I told myself to just suck it up and go. So, I went to this class and look at where I am now. Those thoughts have changed, I have been proven wrong and it shattered the mirrored world that those thoughts came from.

The first day was when I knew it decided how the class flowed, and I liked it. Granted, not surprising considering I’m writing this. And then I got asked this question, or something similar in the first day or the first week, “Why are you taking this class? Is it because it’s a prerequisite that you’re taking it? Or is it because you want to improve your writing?” I took this class because I wanted to improve on my writing skills and I decided that during the first day of the class, the flow of the class was nice, and it didn’t seem overwhelming, you could tell that the professor cared a lot about the students, and you could tell that the professor wasn’t just treating us as students, but instead treating us as adults. That was when I made my decision: to focus on improving my writing skills. I’ve made that my goal and now all that’s left to do is improve.

There are three projects for this class. In the first project, I decided to explore the realm of human emotions because I am always moody. My mood constantly shifts, and I want to explore why that is. I decided to do a research paper style on that topic because it seemed to fit the topic that I was writing about. During the rough draft of the first project, I decided to create the entire essay. Write things down and making sure everything was perfect – to my standards (there were a lot of issues). I am not kidding you when I say I put more time and effort into my rough draft then I did for my final draft. And I had a clue as to why, but it never really clicked until project two.

Project two was different. I wanted to do something that will be related to my future as a Computer Science major. Project two was about the community. I wanted to learn more about the Computer Science community and like in project one, I decided to go towards more of a researchy style paper. One major thing I did differently was that instead of writing everything down and putting it into an “essay” format as I would like to call it. I just put down tad bits of information about this and that over there. And when it came to writing the final draft, I would be forced to sit down and write that mess of information into something that made sense. Because of that technique, every time where I need to do some research about a topic, I will be putting that technique to use. Not only is it helpful to get me to actually edit stuff for my final draft, it also feels good. I still feel that the quality of my project one final draft was super low.

This class focuses a lot about oneself. While it isn’t hard, it does have an impact on the assignments you do in the future. Sure, sometimes you forget to do a reading that was required, but you learn from it. I certainly thought that I would be able to remember everything that was due before class, but I forgot once – and it was most definitely my fault. I simply got too comfortable in this class and forgot my responsibilities that was handed to me because I’m in such a laid-back class. My writing skills has certainly gone up taking this class, especially on things that I lacked such as, imagery and flow. And the things that I didn’t do well on, I learned from it and improved it, because you know what’s good and what’s bad if you reflect on the assignment instead of just taking a letter for measurement.
